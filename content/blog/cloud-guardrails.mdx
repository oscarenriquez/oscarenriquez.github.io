Platform standards fail when they are either too weak to matter or too rigid to survive real product pressure.

The right guardrail is specific at the point of operational risk and invisible everywhere else.

## What deserves standardization

Teams should not repeatedly redesign:

- release workflow basics
- environment boundaries
- secrets handling
- telemetry foundations

These are force multipliers. Standardization here reduces failure modes without flattening product teams into a single template.

## What should remain flexible

Application boundaries, language choices, and domain workflows should stay adaptable unless there is a demonstrated cost to variation.
